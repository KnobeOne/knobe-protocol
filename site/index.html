Open protocol · Plain text · Local verification · v1 frozen

A protocol for knowledge objects that keep their context when they move.

When a document passes to the next person, the next tool, or an AI agent, the words usually arrive, but the things that made them interpretable often do not: where they came from, what was adapted from what, what the author asked, how far they can be trusted. KNOBE (pronounced Nōbee) keeps that context inside the object, in plain text, so it travels with the words.

KNOBE preserves objecthood under compression.

By objecthood: a document remains attached to the context that governs its responsible interpretation: source, transformations, permissions, fidelity limits, adaptation history.

In plain terms

Right now, AI handles knowledge like a port worker unloading containers with no shipping labels: cargo gets dumped on the dock, origin unknown, destination unknown, rules forgotten. Whatever is in the container gets used anyway.

KNOBE is the shipping label that travels inside the cargo itself. When the object reaches a person, a tool, or an AI agent, the label is still there: where it came from, who made it, what conditions governed it, what it can and cannot be used for — without stopping it from moving.

The problem

When this reaches the next person, what comes with it, and what gets left behind?

Knowledge objects move. The context that makes them interpretable usually doesn't.

A graduate student records an oral history. The transcript carries consent terms, a do-not-quote request, and contextual notes. A semester later, an excerpt is pasted into an AI assistant to draft an abstract. The abstract is summarized for a newsletter. A sentence from the newsletter appears in a grant report.

transcript
AI abstract
newsletter
grant report

Every step was reasonable. By the fourth, the words still circulate — but the consent terms, the do-not-quote request, and the notes that governed them are gone, and no one downstream knows they ever existed.

What failed
The words survived. The consent terms and interpretive conditions that governed them did not.

We call this context survivorship bias: the fragment that survives transit is mistaken for the full knowledge object, while the context that made it interpretable disappears. Compression is not the enemy — summary, quotation, translation, and adaptation all depend on it. The problem is compression without portable interpretive obligation: compression makes information portable; context-rich redundancy makes it interpretable. A KNOBE keeps that obligation attached to the object.

The artifact

One file, three layers

A KNOBE is a single plain-text file. A human reads it in any editor. A machine decodes its payload. A verifier checks its payload hash. Here is the whole structure:

--- title: "Oral history — interview 04" spec_version: "1.0" --- # Oral history — interview 04 The human-readable document. Free-form markdown. Opens in any editor, AI assistant, or browser. -----BEGIN KNOBE B64----- eyJzcGVjX3ZlcnNpb24iOiIxLjAiLCJ0aXRsZSI6Ik9yYWwg… # attribution, sources, dHJhbnNmb3JtYXRpb24sIGZpZGVsaXR5LCBzZWFs… # fidelity limits, seal -----END KNOBE B64-----
Hover or tap a layer to see what it carries.
layer 1 · yaml frontmatter
Human-scannable metadata: title, author, license, date.
layer 2 · markdown body
The document itself. No schema. No special software required to read it.
layer 3 · base64 json payload
Attribution, source context, transformation history, fidelity limits, use conditions, accessibility lineage, and a SHA-256 integrity hash.
Yes, it includes metadata. The claim is structural: the metadata travels inside the object, stays inspectable, is hash-sealed, and behaves consistently across tools. A database tag stays in the database. A platform field stays in the platform. A KNOBE carries its context with the object itself.
Context that travels

What the next reader can use

Most provenance systems answer one question: where did this come from? KNOBE asks that too, but also what the next receiver needs to know — what changed, what was adapted, what limits travel with this version, what should not be inferred from it, what conditions the maker wanted preserved.

These fields are optional, but not ornamental. They let a document carry usable context across a gap between people, tools, institutions, or AI systems that do not share the same background.

fidelity_limits
How far the object can be trusted as a representation of its source: what it is fit for, and what must not be inferred from it. A summary that dropped its caveats says so.
use_conditions
The originator's declared terms, carried forward: permitted and disallowed uses, consent and quotation constraints. A condition set at sealing stays legible to a receiver who never met the author.
accessibility
Adaptation lineage. When a captioned, translated, or simplified version is made from a source, this binds it to that source by hash and credits the adapter — so the adaptation stays tied to what it was made from, and to whose labor made it.

These fields inform; they do not enforce. A receiver may ignore them, but the object has still made its interpretive conditions visible. That is the difference between a knowledge object and an orphaned fragment: the fragment can still be quoted, summarized, or fed to a model, but it can no longer say where it came from, what changed, or what it should be trusted for.

Integrity, not truth

The seal reports. The receiver decides.

KNOBE uses standard SHA-256 hashing. There is no cryptographic novelty here, and no claim that a hash can solve trust. The hash proves one narrow thing: the sealed payload has not changed since sealing. It does not prove the content is true, the author is who they say, attribution is honest, consent was obtained, or the object is safe to use. A liar can seal a lie; the seal then proves only that the lie has not changed.

This limit is a design principle, not a flaw to hide. It marks a distinction the rest of the protocol depends on:

control infrastructure

Decides for you

Blocks, permits, denies, enforces, locks, or fails closed. The system makes the call.

responsibility infrastructure

Informs your decision

Makes the relevant conditions visible so a human or institution can decide what to do next. KNOBE is this kind.

The protocol is designed to remain readable by the least-powerful participant in the exchange — which is why it requires no certificate, no registry, no platform, and no authority to verify. Every new or external KNOBE carries a declared trust status — and the protocol's recommended posture is quarantine-first: treat external objects as untrusted until reviewed, regardless of whether they verify. A KNOBE can verify and still remain in quarantine. Verification means the payload is intact, not that the object is true, safe, or appropriate. The green check is where inspection begins, not where it ends.

Proof

Verify any KNOBE

You do not need to understand the code to verify a KNOBE — it reports a small set of plain, human-readable states.

lens.py is the reference verifier: a single standard-library Python file with no external dependencies. It runs locally — it does not upload your file, call an AI model, or contact a server. It extracts the payload, computes the canonical hash, and reports an integrity status and a conformance level. No server, no AI, no certificate authority — pure math. Prefer no terminal? KNOBE Lens runs the same algorithm in your browser: drag in a file, get a result locally.

$ python3 lens.py interview-04.knobe.md
title: Oral history — interview 04
quarantine_status: quarantine
status: verified
body_hash: match
integrity verified. Integrity is not truth — inspect before trusting.

Lens and lens.py report three things: an integrity status (is the sealed record intact?), whether the visible body was sealed and unchanged, and a conformance level against the v1 spec. The full state definitions live on the Spec and Lens pages. The important point here is simpler: a verified KNOBE means the sealed record is intact — not that its contents are true or safe.

The first proving ground

Accessibility is not a metaphor here

The KNOBE instructional approach will be piloted in multiple large courses at UC Davis, ranging from 100 to 500 students. Accessibility is not an add-on use case for KNOBE; it is the first proving ground, with its own ethical stakes, and it reveals the general problem most clearly.

When materials are captioned, translated, simplified, or remediated, the adaptation is knowledge work. It carries labor, judgment, and responsibility. But adaptations usually circulate severed from their sources: the adapted object travels without the original, without the adapter's contribution recorded, without a clear account of what changed or what fidelity limits remain. The labor disappears, and the link to the source disappears with it.

A KNOBE keeps the adaptation bound to its source by hash and credits the adapter in the record. This page links to a worked example: a plain-language simplification of the white paper that records what it was adapted from, who adapted it, and what it does and does not preserve. It is the vision in miniature: knowledge made legible across a gap between parties who do not share the same access to it, with the labor of making that crossing possible kept visible.

Same KNOBE, many ways in. That is one reason accessibility collaborators have been among the first people to recognize the value of the protocol. A KNOBE lets everyone receive and return the same knowledge object without requiring everyone to use the same interface. It can be read as plain text, inspected by a verifier, opened with a screen reader, transformed into audio, adapted into a visual layout, summarized by an AI assistant, or used inside a learning platform. The object remains the same, and its context, lineage, and verification record remain attached.

For agent builders

Agent harness engineering, not prompt engineering

Prompt engineering governs what you ask an agent. Agent harness engineering is the broader discipline — execution policy, tool permissions, memory, observability: the conditions around the agent. KNOBE comes at the problem from the other side. It does not engineer the system; it engineers what the object carries through any system. Harness infrastructure governs the conditions around the agent. KNOBE governs the conditions carried by the artifact.

harness infrastructure

Controls the encounter

Execution policy, tool permissions, observability, memory: the conditions around the agent.

KNOBE · object layer

Preserves the object

Attribution, transformation history, fidelity limits: carried by the artifact across encounters.

If the artifact arrives stripped, no harness can reconstruct what was never carried. One honest limit: KNOBE carries the human-supplied context around AI-mediated work — sources, transformations, permissions, fidelity limits. It does not open the model's reasoning. It solves context survival across AI-mediated exchange, not AI interpretability.

Related work

The gap KNOBE fills

KNOBE did not invent the knowledge object, the self-verifying file, or the idea that metadata should travel with content. A lot of good work moves in the same direction — which is evidence the problem is real. KNOBE's design point is narrower: can a single plain-text document carry enough declared context to be interpreted responsibly after it leaves its original setting? That puts it beside, but not inside, several neighbors:

C2PAProvenance and integrity for media, backed by signing authorities and certificates. KNOBE is document-centered, plain-text, and authority-free at the open layer; credential-backed attestation can be added later but is never required to verify.
NanopublicationsSmall, citable, provenance-rich claims for the Semantic Web. KNOBE aims at the human web: one readable markdown file, no RDF or specialized infrastructure required.
RO-Crate / PROVPackage research objects and describe provenance for reproducibility and FAIR data. KNOBE's concern is narrower: whether one traveling object carries the context a receiver needs.
Git / OpenTimestampsTrack versions, prove existence, and anchor files in external systems. KNOBE can compose with them, but its seal travels inside the object, even when the object leaves the repo.
Agent-readable knowledge formatsOrganize portable markdown for human–agent exchange. KNOBE's concern is the interpretive obligation carried by the object itself: source, transformation history, fidelity limits, use conditions, and trust posture.

The neighbors make artifacts findable, attributable, reproducible, attestable, or agent-readable. KNOBE's narrower job is to keep interpretive context attached to the object itself, in plain text, after it moves beyond the system that first held it. The white paper situates KNOBE among these in detail.

Honest limits

What KNOBE is not

KNOBE is not a platform, a system of record, a truth machine, DRM, a smart contract, identity verification, or a replacement for institutional governance. It does not open the AI black box, and it is not finished.

It does not solve hallucination, copyright, authorship, or governance. Attribution and identity are declared in v1, not cryptographically proven. KNOBEs do not execute: a .knobe.md is inert plain text — it carries obligations; it does not enforce them. And it applies equally to work done with AI, with some AI, or with none — the format records what actually happened, whatever that was.

KNOBE in use

The tools that read, build, and carry KNOBEs

The protocol is the open layer. Around it, a small set of tools makes KNOBEs usable in practice — from verifying a file, to authoring one, to working with a whole collection. Two are available now; the rest are in development.

Questions

FAQ

Is this just metadata?

It includes metadata, but the claim is structural: the metadata travels inside the object as plain text, stays human-readable and inspectable, is hash-sealed, and behaves consistently across any tool. A vector-database tag or a course-system field stays behind when the file moves. A KNOBE's payload moves with it, including the fields written for the receiver, like fidelity limits and adaptation lineage.

What does the hash prove?

That the payload is unaltered since sealing. Nothing else. Not accuracy, not honesty, not safety. A liar can seal a lie; the seal then faithfully proves the lie has not been edited. Verification is where inspection begins.

Does this require AI?

No. KNOBE applies to work done with AI, with some AI, or with none. The planned pilots span a range: some courses disallow AI entirely, others allow limited self-attested use. The Magic Grove builds a KNOBE with no AI at all. The format records what actually happened in whatever workflow occurred.

Does it replace the course system, ethics board, or repository?

No. It is a system of context, not a system of record. It carries attribution and interpretive obligation between the systems you already have, and lowers the cost of moving between them.

What does "seed stage" mean?

v1 file semantics are frozen — the spec will not break existing files. The ecosystem around KNOBE is not finished. Extensions may add optional fields; breaking changes require a v2.0 declaration. We are inviting testing, critique, and independent implementation.

Governance

An open protocol, stewarded in the open

Open and staying open
KNOBE Protocol v1 is authored and stewarded by David Kyle, University of California, Davis. It is released under CC BY 4.0 and will remain open. Anyone may implement, extend, or build on it; the protocol itself will not be enclosed.
v1 is frozen
The canonical hash rule, marker format, required fields, three-layer structure, and verification semantics will not change in v1.x. Backward-compatible additions may be issued as v1.x updates. Breaking changes require a v2.0 declaration. Files sealed under v1.0 remain valid under any v1.x verifier.
Semantics follow the sealed version
A verifier must apply the verification semantics of the spec version a file was sealed under. A future verifier may report additional advisory information, but must not reinterpret a previously valid KNOBE as failed.
How the spec changes
Proposed changes, bug reports, and implementation questions are submitted through the public source repository. The steward reviews proposals, maintains a public comment period for substantive changes, and issues decisions with documented rationale.
Team and advisory board
KNOBE is developed with a growing team at UC Davis spanning research, accessibility, software development, and education technology. An advisory board is forming — we are looking for people with experience in independent protocol implementation, educational technology, accessibility, open infrastructure, and institutional knowledge governance. Write to djkyle@ucdavis.edu with a brief note on your perspective and relevant work. There is no deadline; this is an ongoing invitation.
