We're now in an era where knowledge moves continuously through humans and AI. AI has changed the scale and speed of information movement so dramatically that we can no longer assume context will be reconstructed downstream as knowledge passes through multiple systems, people, and tools.

The missing infrastructure is not simply better AI. Nor is the answer to pretend knowledge can be made pure again by keeping it outside AI systems. Knowledge has always moved, compressed, adapted, and changed. The challenge now is to make that movement more visible, responsible, and inspectable.

KNOBE is an open protocol for auditable plain-text knowledge objects that carry their source, history, limits, and obligations across human and AI systems. Accessibility, interoperability, portability.

What is lost when knowledge moves

Knowledge objects move. The context that makes them interpretable usually doesn't.

A graduate student records an oral history. The transcript carries consent terms, a do-not-quote request, and contextual notes. A semester later, an excerpt is pasted into an AI assistant to draft an abstract. The abstract is summarized for a newsletter. A sentence from the newsletter appears in a grant report.

Every step was reasonable. By the fourth, the words still circulate — but the consent terms, the do-not-quote request, and the notes that governed them are gone, and no one downstream knows they ever existed.

What failed
The words survived. The consent terms and interpretive conditions that governed them did not.
The infrastructure gap
Software has Git

Software history is preserved through version control, commits, diffs, and inspectable change records.

Knowledge lacks an ordinary portable equivalent

Knowledge work has no equally ordinary habit for carrying the history of an idea as it moves through systems.

The AI boundary

When knowledge crosses an AI boundary, it is stripped of its origin, fidelity limits, and context. You get the content, but lose the object.

What KNOBE changes

Knowledge work is most fragile at handoff points: email, shared drives, LMS exports, AI assistants, accessibility adaptations, repositories, reports. The words may survive, but the conditions that make them responsible to use often do not.

KNOBE makes context portable. A .knobe.md carries its declared provenance, adaptations, fidelity limits, use conditions, accessibility lineage, and verification record in one readable file.

  • 1Context travels with the work. Every .knobe.md carries its source, transformations, consent, fidelity limits, and accessibility lineage inside the file.
  • 2Verify integrity locally. Anyone can check whether the sealed record is intact with lens.py or Lens — no account, registry, certificate authority, or server required.
  • 3Obligations show up where they are needed. The payload fields are written for the next receiver: what changed, what is safe to infer, what should not be done, and who adapted what.
  • 4The seal informs; it never locks. A broken or mismatched seal is a cue to inspect, not a barrier to reading. The file stays plain text for accessibility, education, and research use.
  • 5One object, many interfaces. The same KNOBE can move through screen readers, AI assistants, LMSes, repositories, local folders, and plain-text editors without losing its record.
  • 6Start simple. Build bigger later. KNOBEs work in email, shared drives, and local folders today, and can later be indexed or composed inside Git, RO-Crate, FDO, OKF, or other infrastructure.

One file, three layers

A KNOBE is a single plain-text file. A human reads it in any editor. A machine decodes its payload. A verifier checks its payload hash.

layer 1 · yaml frontmatter
Human-scannable metadata: title, author, license, date.
layer 2 · markdown body
The document itself. No schema. No special software required to read it.
layer 3 · base64 json payload
Attribution, source context, transformation history, fidelity limits, use conditions, accessibility lineage, and a SHA-256 integrity hash.
